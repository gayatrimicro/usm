Valve - E - VAL

The E-VAL fills valve bags with powders or granular materials of small particulate size. Wheat and potato starches, PVC powders, mineral powders, feed meals, screenings, are typical products which can be packed at speed, with accuracy. The packer is particularly beneficial on high-value products, and will give rapid payback on initial capital investment. Depending on the product 500 bags/minute can be obtained from a single packer. Higher Outputs are achieved by installing machines side by side. The E-VAL has been designed to bulk manual and automatic bag placing.
BENEFITS
  • Non-degradative handling of products
  • Rapid changeover to other bag sizes
  • Low levels of dust in operation
  • Optimum accuracy in bag filling
  • Low pressure filling to prevent damage to valve bags
  • Produces consistent bag-shaped bags ready for pallets and transportation.
METHOD OF OPERATION
An empty valve bag is placed ( either manually or automatically ) on the filling spout 6 and gripped by the bag clamp 8. the in feed gate 3 is opened and material flows from the supply hopper into the Air chamber 2. the gate is controlled by a level switch 1. Air chamber pressurization is controlled by pinch valves 4 and 5. the introduction of compressed air forces material at full flow through pinch valve 7 into the bag. When the control system initiates dribble flow, the material flow is reduced by partial closure of the valve. At target weight achievement the valve is closed. The gag clamp opens and the filled bag can be ejected (either manually or automatically ) from the bag support 9 for subsequent transportation 10. after weighing has been completed the air pressure in the chamber is relieved and the in feed gate opens. New material can flow in and next filling process commences.
 
DESIGN
The pressure flow packing system comprises the valve bag packing machine, the microprocessor control system, and a low-pressure compressor. Weight of the filled bag is transduced by an overload protected load cell from forces exerted on a parallel lever system. Long-term weighing accuracy is assured by flexure suspension and check link restraints.
