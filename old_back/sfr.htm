Valve - SFR 

Gravity Packer for Valve Bags SFR
 
 
The SFR fills valve bags with free flowing materials such as polyethylene granulates, grain, fertilizer, salt, granulated chemical products etc. High output filling, optimum product compaction in the bag, regular box shaped bags, and low energy requirements are proven advantages of the SFR. Twin spout systems can achieve outputs of up to 1400 bags/hour.
The success of the system is demonstrable by hundreds of installations worldwide.
The incorporation of an automatic bag placer will minimize operator involvement a particular benefit when bagging hazardous products.
BENEFITS
  • High bagging output
  • Low energy requirement
  • Optimum bag filling
  • Secure valve sealing by head first bag ejection
  • Rapid changeover to other bag sizes
  • Produces box-shaped bags ready for pallets and transportation
 
OPERATION
Product to be bagged is weighed in a Net Weigher
  1. Once a bag is placed on the bag clamp
  2. The weigh hopper doors are opened to release the material through an adjustable feed gate
  3. Down the filling spout into the bag. Throughout the filling process the bag is supported
  4. And the integral posing mechanism
  5. Will distribute/ compact product in the bag. The filled bag is automatically released; head first ejection onto the conveyor belt
  6. Secure seals the valve.
Design
Our gravity packer guarantee long-term high performance, through:
  • adjustable feed gate control
  • optimal material flow
  • controlled air displacement
  • head first ejection of filled bags
Easy operation
Valve bags are securely and reliably placed using an expandable bag clamp. Outputs of up to 1400 bags/ hour can be achieved on twin spout systems., with only one operator.
Rapid changeover
Varying bag sizes are accommodated with minimal delay by means of simple adjustments.
Low energy requirement
The SFR design utilizes gravitational forces to accelerate material flow into the bag, requiring only minimal energy to achieve optimum filling.
Dust free filling
The special design of the bag clamp ensures a secure seal during filling. Displaced air is drawn of by means of a suction hood.

Out Put
Output is dependent upon product characteristic, target weight and valve dimensions.
Higher outputs can be achieved with multi-spout systems.
